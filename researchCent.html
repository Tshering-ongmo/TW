Research instruments, equipment, and facilities at Centre for Science and Environment Research (CSER), Sherubtse College.

In a Water Chemistry Laboratory, researchers can conduct diverse studies related to water quality, pollution, and treatment technologies. Key tasks include monitoring physical and chemical parameters such as pH, dissolved oxygen, and nutrient levels in surface and groundwater. The lab is also used to analyze pollutants like heavy metals, pesticides, pharmaceuticals, and microplastics, supporting toxicological studies on their environmental impact.

Researchers can explore the efficiency of wastewater treatment systems and develop advanced purification technologies using nanomaterials or biofilters. Furthermore, the lab facilitates studies on nutrient cycling, climate change effects on water bodies, and sustainable water management practices.
